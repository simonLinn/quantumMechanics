Numerically Solving of the Infinite Quantum Well¶


    Project#1

  • Consider an $1D$ Infinite Quantum Well :
    • $$ V(x)= \begin{cases} 0,& 0 \leq x \leq L \\ \infty,& x<0 , x>L \end{cases} $$ $$L = 5 nm = 50 \mathring{A} , \Delta x = 2.5 \mathring{A} $$

  • time independent Schrdinger Equation(TISE)

    • $$ -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{d^2}{d x^2} \psi(x) + V(x)\psi(x) = E\psi(x)$$

  • Discretization of TISE:

    • Turn continuous space $x$ into a discrete number of steps , $N$ .

    Than get $N$ values $x_i$ , $h = \Delta x = x_{i+1} - x_i$ .

    Assume $\Delta x$ is a constant and small enough.

    $$ \frac{d \psi(x)}{dx} = \lim_{\Delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\psi(x+\Delta x) - \psi(x)}{\Delta x} \approx \frac{\psi(x+h) - \psi(x)}{x_{i+1} - x_i} $$

    $$ \frac{d^2 \psi(x)}{dx^2} = \frac{d}{dx} \frac{d \psi(x)}{dx} = \frac {\frac{\psi(x_{i+1})-\psi(x_i)}{\Delta x}-\frac{\psi(x_i)-\psi(x_{i-1})}{\Delta x} }{\Delta x} $$
    $$=\frac{\psi(x_{i+1})-2\psi(x_i)+\psi(x_{i-1})}{\Delta x^2}$$

    So the TISE can be discretized as


    $$ -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \frac{\psi(x_{i+1})-2\psi(x_i)+\psi(x_{i-1})}{\Delta x^2} + 0 * \psi(x)= E\psi(x)$$

    $$\Longrightarrow \frac{-\hbar^2}{2 m \Delta x^2} \begin{pmatrix} -2 & 1 & 0 & \ddots & \ddots& 0 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 & 0 & \ddots& 0 \\ 0& 1 & -2 & 1 & \ddots& 0 \\\ddots & \ddots & \ddots & \ddots & 1 & 0\\ 0 & \ddots& \ddots& \ddots & 1 & -2 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} \psi_1 \\ \psi_2 \\ \ddots \\ \ddots \\ \psi_{N-1}\end{pmatrix} = E \begin{pmatrix} \psi_1 \\ \psi_2 \\ \ddots \\ \ddots \\ \psi_{N-1}\end{pmatrix} $$

    After discretization , TISE becomes an eigenvalues promble , and eigenvalues $E$ the eigenvectors $\vec{\psi}$ can be solved .

import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

HBAR = 6.62607004 * (10**-34)
N = 21
L = 5. * (10**-9)
DeltaX=2.5 * (10**-10)
M=9.10938356*  (10**-31)
squareMatrix=(np.diag(np.ones(N-1),-1) -2* np.diag(np.ones(N),0) + np.diag(np.ones(N-1),1))
x = np.linspace(0.,L,N)

H=np.true_divide(-1*np.power(HBAR,2),(2*M*np.power(DeltaX,2)))*squareMatrix
E,psi = np.linalg.eigh(H)
psi=psi.T
